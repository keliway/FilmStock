Version 2.0 — Updated February 2026

What's New in Version 2.0

Version 2.0 is a major update that changes how FilmStock tracks your film stock. Rolls are now individual entities — each roll you own can have its own expiry date, frozen status, exposure count, and notes. Your existing data has been automatically migrated to this new model.

Migration note: If you previously had a film with multiple expiry dates (e.g., you entered several dates for the same format), the app created individual rolls for each date. In some edge cases, expiry dates from migrated data may appear as a 6-digit string (e.g., 022007 instead of 02/2007). The app handles these correctly, but you can clean them up manually — see Fixing migrated expiry dates below.
Roll-by-Roll Tracking — Each roll is a distinct item. Group them by format, expiry date, and frozen status.
Redesigned Film Detail — Rolls are displayed grouped by format and expiry date. Edit each group individually.
Export & Import (Beta) — Export your film inventory as JSON or CSV. Import inventory from a file.
Notes Per Roll Group — Add free-text notes to each roll group (e.g., "from Japan batch").
Camera Format — Assign a film format to each camera. The app auto-selects the best-matching camera when you load a film.
Multiple Expiry Chips — If a film has rolls with different expiry dates, all dates appear as chips in the list view.
Image Catalog Search — Search for film card images by name or manufacturer when choosing from the catalog.

Getting Started

Adding Your First Film

  1. Tap the + button in the My Films tab.
  2. Fill in the Film Information section: manufacturer, name, type, and ISO speed.
  3. Optionally add a film card image (tap the camera icon or browse the catalog).
  4. In the Rolls section, tap Add Roll Batch to specify what you have:
    • Choose a format (35mm, 120, etc.)
    • Set the quantity
    • Optionally set expiry date, frozen status, number of exposures, and notes
  5. Add more roll batches if you have the same film in different formats or with different expiry dates.
  6. Tap Save.

Adding More Rolls to an Existing Film

  1. Open the film from your collection.
  2. Scroll past the roll groups and tap Add Rolls.
  3. Configure the new roll batch (format, quantity, expiry, frozen, exposures, notes).
  4. Tap Add.

Editing a Roll Group

Each roll group (e.g., "3x 35mm · 12/2026 · frozen") has its own edit button on the right.

  1. Open a film's detail view.
  2. Tap the pencil icon on the roll group you want to change.
  3. Adjust the quantity, expiry date, frozen status, exposures, or notes.
  4. Tap Save.

To remove rolls entirely, set the quantity to 0 and save, or reduce the quantity as you use them up.

Fixing Migrated Expiry Dates

If you upgraded from an older version and had films with multiple expiry dates, those rolls were automatically migrated. In some cases the expiry date might appear in a compact 6-digit format (e.g., 022007). The app understands this format, but if you'd like it to look cleaner:

  1. Open the film in the detail view.
  2. Tap the pencil icon on the affected roll group.
  3. Clear the expiry date field and re-enter it in the standard format: MM/YYYY (e.g., 02/2007).
  4. Tap Save.

Taking Custom Film Photos

  1. When adding or editing a film, tap the camera icon.
  2. Choose Take Picture or Choose from Library.
  3. Frame your film box.
  4. The app automatically crops to a square format — adjust the crop if needed.
  5. Tap Done to save.

You can also browse the built-in image catalog using Choose from Catalog and search by manufacturer or film name.

Loading a Film in Your Camera

  1. Open a film from your collection and tap Load Film.
  2. Select the format you want to load (if the film has multiple formats).
  3. For roll formats, choose which specific roll to load — rolls are grouped by expiry date, frozen status, and exposures.
  4. Select a camera. If you've assigned a format to your cameras, the best-matching camera is highlighted (or auto-selected if there's only one match).
  5. Optionally adjust the shot ISO (shown in orange if different from the film's native speed).
  6. Tap Load.

Managing Cameras

  1. Go to the Loaded Films tab.
  2. Tap the camera icon in the top left to open Manage Cameras.
  3. Tap + to add a new camera — you can set a name and assign a film format.
  4. Tap an existing camera to edit its name or format.
  5. Assigning a format helps the app auto-select and group cameras when loading film.

Managing Finished Rolls

  1. Go to the Loaded Films tab and switch to the Finished Rolls segment.
  2. Swipe on a roll to update development status:
    • To develop — Waiting to be developed
    • In development — Currently at the lab
    • Developed — Completed
  3. Tap a finished roll to see full details: which roll was used, loading date, camera, frozen status, shot ISO, and notes.
  4. Swipe to Re-load if you need to move it back to loaded films.

Exporting Your Inventory

  1. Go to Settings and tap Export or Import Inventory.
  2. Choose Export and select a format — JSON (detailed, machine-readable) or CSV (spreadsheet-friendly).
  3. Tap Export — a share sheet will appear so you can save to Files, send via email, etc.

Note: The export includes your film inventory only (not loaded films, cameras, or finished rolls). Film card pictures are not embedded in the file.

Importing Inventory

  1. Go to Settings → Export or Import Inventory.
  2. Switch to the Import tab and tap Choose File.
  3. Select a previously exported JSON or CSV file.
  4. Review the import preview — it shows which films will be added.
  5. Tap Import to confirm.

After import, the app will attempt to automatically match film card images from the built-in catalog based on film name and manufacturer. If the manufacturer name in the import file doesn't match exactly (e.g., "Fuji" vs. "Fujifilm"), you can fix it by editing the film after import.

Using the Widget

  1. Long press on your home screen and tap +.
  2. Search for FilmStock and choose a widget size.
  3. Tap arrows on the widget to cycle through currently loaded films.
  4. The widget shows ISO in orange if you're shooting at a non-native ISO.

Entering Expiry Dates

When adding or editing rolls, you can enter expiry dates in several formats. The app auto-formats your input as you type:

  • Year only: 4 digits — e.g., 2025
  • Month and year: 6 digits — e.g., 112025 becomes 11/2025
  • Full date: 8 digits — e.g., 11152025 becomes 11/15/2025

Expired rolls are highlighted in red in the film detail view and in the list view chips. You can enable expiry date chips in Settings → My Films → Show Expiry Date in Chip. If a film has multiple expiry dates across its rolls, up to three chips are shown, with a +N overflow badge for the rest.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does FilmStock require an account or internet connection?
A: No. FilmStock works completely offline. All your data is stored locally on your device. No account, no login, no cloud sync required.
Q: What changed in version 2.0 — will I lose my data?
A: Your data is fully preserved. Version 2.0 introduces a roll-centric model: each roll in your inventory is now a distinct item with its own expiry date, frozen status, and notes. When you update, the app automatically migrates your existing films into this new structure. If you previously had a film with multiple expiry dates, individual rolls are created for each date.
Q: Some of my expiry dates look like "022007" instead of "02/2007". How do I fix this?
A: This can happen with rolls that were migrated from an older data format. The app handles these dates correctly, but if you'd like the display to look cleaner, open the film detail, tap the pencil icon on the affected roll group, clear the expiry date, and re-enter it as MM/YYYY (e.g., 02/2007). Tap Save.
Q: How do I track multiple rolls of the same film with different expiry dates?
A: Add each batch of rolls separately. When adding a film, tap Add Roll Batch for each group (e.g., 3x 35mm expiring 12/2026 and 2x 35mm expiring 06/2024). In the film detail view, each group appears as its own row, grouped by format. You can edit any group independently.
Q: What does the camera format setting do?
A: You can assign a film format (e.g., 35mm, 120) to each camera. When you load a film, the app uses this to:
  • Auto-select the camera if there's exactly one camera matching the film's format
  • Show matching cameras in a "Suggested" section at the top of the camera list when there are multiple options
Set a camera's format in Loaded Films → camera icon → tap the camera.
Q: Can I import my collection from another app or spreadsheet?
A: Yes — via the CSV import feature (Settings → Export or Import Inventory). The CSV format is documented within the export file itself. Prepare a file with columns for name, manufacturer, type, ISO, format, quantity, expiry date, frozen status, and optional notes. After import, film card images can be matched from the built-in catalog or added manually.
Q: The manufacturer I imported doesn't match the catalog — how do I fix it?
A: Open the film, tap the ··· menu and choose Edit Film. Tap the manufacturer row to open the picker and select the correct manufacturer. Tap the back button and then Save. The app will re-run auto-image detection with the corrected name.
Q: What does shooting at a different ISO mean?
A: When loading a film, you can change the ISO to indicate you're rating it differently (e.g., pushing or pulling). The app shows this ISO in orange to remind you it's different from the film's native speed.
Q: Can I track sheet film quantities?
A: Yes. Large format sheet films (4x5, 5x7, 8x10) appear in a dedicated Sheets section in the film detail view, separate from roll formats. You can load individual sheets and track quantities just like rolls. Other custom formats appear in an Other section.
Q: What is the "frozen" status?
A: Marking a roll as frozen indicates you're storing it in a freezer to preserve it. This is tracked per roll group and shown as a chip in the film detail view and in loaded and finished roll details.
Q: Is my data backed up?
A: Your FilmStock data is included in your iPhone's iCloud backup (if backup is enabled in Settings). If you restore your iPhone from a backup, your collection, cameras, and settings will be restored. You can also export your inventory manually as a JSON or CSV file for an extra copy.
Q: What does "Buy Me a Coffee" do?
A: It's an optional one-time tip to support continued development. All features remain free regardless. Thank you!
Q: What happens if I delete a camera that has finished rolls?
A: The app stores a snapshot of the camera name when you finish a roll. Your finished rolls will still show which camera was used even after you delete it.
Q: What's the difference between "Remove" and "Finish Roll"?
A: Finish Roll marks the film as shot and moves it to Finished Rolls, reducing your inventory count. Remove is for accidental loads — it removes the entry and returns the roll to your inventory without recording it as finished.
Q: Why doesn't my widget show film images?
A: Make sure you have at least one film loaded in a camera. The widget only displays currently loaded films. If images still don't appear, remove and re-add the widget, or restart your iPhone.
Q: How do I customize which formats appear in the table view?
A: Go to Settings → My Films → Enabled Formats. You can also add custom format names here.

Troubleshooting

Migrated expiry dates showing as 6-digit numbers

  • This is a cosmetic issue from the v2.0 data migration. Dates like 022007 mean February 2007 — the app sorts and highlights them correctly.
  • To fix the display: open the film detail → tap the pencil on the roll group → clear the expiry field and re-enter as MM/YYYY → Save.

Imported film doesn't get the right image automatically

  • Auto-image detection matches based on film name and manufacturer. If the manufacturer name in your import file differs from the built-in catalog (e.g., "Fuji" vs. "Fujifilm"), the match won't be found.
  • Fix it by editing the film (··· → Edit Film → change manufacturer) and saving.
  • Alternatively, add the image manually via the camera icon in Edit Film.

Film card image missing after import

  • Custom film card photos are not embedded in export files.
  • After importing, add your custom photos manually by tapping Edit Film and selecting the image.
  • Built-in catalog images will be detected automatically based on name and manufacturer.

Camera not auto-selected when loading film

  • Auto-selection only works when exactly one camera has a format matching the film being loaded.
  • If you have multiple cameras with the same format, you'll need to select manually — matching cameras appear at the top under "Suggested".
  • Assign formats to your cameras via Loaded Films → camera icon → tap camera.

Roll group not appearing in film detail

  • Roll groups with a quantity of 0 are hidden — they've been used up.
  • If you accidentally reduced a quantity to 0, tap Add Rolls at the bottom of the detail view to add it back.

Widget not updating

  • Remove the widget and add it again.
  • Make sure you have films loaded in cameras.
  • Restart your iPhone.
  • Check that background app refresh is enabled in Settings for FilmStock.

Camera or photo library permission denied

  • Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Camera (or Photos) and enable access for FilmStock.

App crashes or freezes

  • Make sure you're running the latest version from the App Store.
  • Restart your iPhone.
  • Check available storage on your device.
  • If the issue persists, contact support with your iOS version and app version.
