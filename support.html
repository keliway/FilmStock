Getting Started

Adding Your First Film

  1. Tap the + button in the My Films tab
  2. Select a manufacturer (e.g., Kodak, Ilford, Fuji)
  3. Enter the film name (e.g., Portra 400, HP5 Plus)
  4. The app automatically detects film metadata and loads catalog images
  5. Optionally add expiry date, quantity, format, and comments
  6. Tap Save

Taking Custom Film Photos

  1. When adding or editing a film, tap the camera icon
  2. Choose Take Picture or Choose from Library
  3. Frame your film box photo
  4. The app automatically crops to a square format
  5. Adjust the crop if needed
  6. Tap Done to save

Loading a Film in Your Camera

  1. Open any film from your collection
  2. Tap Load Film
  3. Select a camera or add a new one
  4. Optionally adjust the ISO (defaults to film's native speed)
  5. View loaded films in the Loaded Films tab
  6. Tap Finish Roll when you're done shooting

Managing Finished Rolls

  1. Go to Loaded Films tab
  2. Switch to Finished Rolls segment
  3. Swipe on a roll to update development status:
    • To develop - Waiting to be developed
    • In development - Currently at the lab
    • Developed - Completed
  4. Swipe to Re-load if you need to move it back to loaded films
  5. Use filters to sort by status, camera, format, or date

Using the Widget

  1. Long press on your home screen
  2. Tap the + button in the top corner
  3. Search for FilmStock
  4. Choose widget size (small, medium, or large)
  5. Add to home screen
  6. Tap arrows on the widget to cycle through loaded films
  7. Widget displays ISO speed (in orange if different from native)

Managing Cameras

  1. Go to Loaded Films tab
  2. Tap the camera icon in the top left
  3. Add, edit, or delete cameras
  4. Camera names persist even if the camera is deleted from finished rolls

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does FilmStock require an account or internet connection?
A: No! FilmStock works completely offline. All your data is stored locally on your device. No account, no login, no cloud sync.
Q: What does shooting at a different ISO mean?
A: When loading a film, you can change the ISO to indicate you're rating it differently (e.g., pushing or pulling). The app shows this ISO in orange to remind you it's different from the film's native speed. This is useful when you need to adjust for lighting conditions or creative effect.
Q: How do I customize which formats appear in table view?
A: Go to Settings ‚Üí My Films ‚Üí Enabled Formats. The table view will show all your enabled formats with horizontal scrolling. You can also add custom formats here.
Q: What happens if I delete a camera that has finished rolls?
A: The app stores a snapshot of the camera name when you finish a roll, so your finished rolls will still show which camera was used even if you later delete that camera.
Q: Can I track sheet film quantities?
A: Yes! For large format sheet films (4√ó5, 5√ó7, 8√ó10), you can load individual sheets and unload them one at a time using the swipe actions in Loaded Films.
Q: How do I add custom film images?
A: When adding or editing a film, tap the camera icon. You can take a new photo or choose from your library. The app automatically crops images to square format and stores them locally.
Q: Can I track multiple rolls of the same film with different expiry dates?
A: Yes! When viewing a film's details, you'll see all formats grouped by expiry date. Each combination of format and expiry date is tracked separately.
Q: What does "Buy Me a Coffee" do?
A: It's an optional one-time tip/donation to support continued development. All features remain free regardless of whether you purchase it. It's simply a way to say thank you!
Q: How do I enter expiry dates?
A: You can enter expiry dates in three formats:
  • Year only: Type 4 digits (e.g., 2025)
  • Month and year: Type 6 digits (e.g., 112025 becomes 11/2025)
  • Full date: Type 8 digits (e.g., 11152025 becomes 11/15/2025)
The app automatically formats and validates your input.
Q: Why doesn't my widget show film images?
A: Make sure you have at least one film loaded in a camera. The widget only displays films that are currently loaded, not your entire collection. If images still don't appear, try removing and re-adding the widget.
Q: How do I use filters and sorting?
A: Tap the filter icon to access filtering options. You can filter by manufacturer, type, format, speed ranges, cameras, and development status. The app remembers your filter and sort preferences between sessions.
Q: What's the difference between "Remove" and "Finish Roll"?
A: Finish Roll marks the film as completed and moves it to Finished Rolls, decreasing your inventory. Remove is for accidental loads - it removes the loaded film entry and restores the quantity to your collection without recording it as finished.
Q: Will FilmStock support iPad?
A: Currently FilmStock is optimized for iPhone, but it will run on iPad in compatibility mode. Native iPad support may come in a future update based on user feedback.
Q: Is my data backed up?
A: Your FilmStock data is included in your iPhone's iCloud backup (if enabled). If you restore your iPhone from an iCloud backup, your film collection, photos, cameras, and all settings will be restored.

Troubleshooting

Widget not updating

  • Remove the widget and add it again
  • Make sure you have films loaded in cameras (not just in collection)
  • Restart your iPhone
  • Check that the app has background refresh enabled in Settings

Camera permission denied

  • Go to Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Camera
  • Enable camera access for FilmStock

Photo library permission denied

  • Go to Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Photos
  • Enable photo library access for FilmStock

Cropped image doesn't match what I selected

  • The app automatically crops to square format for consistency
  • Use pinch-to-zoom and drag to adjust the crop area before tapping Done
  • Make sure you're viewing the entire square overlay when taking camera photos

App crashes or freezes

  • Make sure you're running the latest version from the App Store
  • Restart your iPhone
  • Check available storage space on your device
  • If the issue persists, contact support (see below)

Film images not appearing

  • The app includes catalog images for popular films
  • For custom or rare films, you'll need to add your own photo
  • Make sure you've granted photo library access if selecting existing photos
  • Custom images are stored locally in the app's documents folder

Filters or settings not persisting

  • Filter and sort preferences are now saved automatically
  • If they reset, try force-quitting the app and relaunching
  • Make sure you have the latest app version
